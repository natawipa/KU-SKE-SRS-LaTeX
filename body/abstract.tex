\chapter*{Abstract}
\label{chap:abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{\nameref{chap:abstract}}

Real-Time Figure Skating Jump Recognition using Computer Vision and Pose-Based LSTM (Figure Flow) is an AI-powered system designed to automatically detect and label figure skating jumps in real-time during live broadcasts and video replays. By combining computer vision and sequence modeling, our system addresses the challenges faced by casual viewers, non-expert commentators, and even judges who struggle to distinguish between subtle jump differences such as toe loop versus Salchow takeoffs. 

The system employs YOLO for skater detection, MediaPipe Pose for keypoint extraction, and LSTM for temporal motion analysis to classify jumps into toe jumps (toe loop, flip, Lutz) and edge jumps (Salchow, loop, Axel). Unlike existing manual video review processes and post-event analysis tools, Figure Flow provides instant on-screen overlays with jump identification and simple explanations, making figure skating accessible to global audiences.
